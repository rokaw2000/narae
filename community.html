Ways to Be Part

Whether you are seeking care, offering support, or exploring collaboration — there is a place for you in naræ.

At the center

The heart of naræ

naræ supports immigrant and culturally diverse women navigating breast and gynecologic cancers.

Every part of naræ is built around them.

A thoughtful foundation

Built with care, not haste

This work is shaped slowly, with attention to trust, cultural understanding, and the quality of each connection.

The goal is not to gather widely, but to build something sincere, steady, and lasting. It is meant to grow with integrity, not urgency.

naræ Roles

Who belongs here.

naræ Fellow →
An immigrant or culturally diverse woman navigating breast or gynecologic cancer, carrying not only a diagnosis but also the weight of language, migration, family roles, and the experience of feeling unseen. She comes to naræ to be welcomed fully, as she is. She is at the heart of why naræ exists.
naræ Companion →
A Fellow who, in her own time, chooses to walk beside another woman at the beginning. She understands the isolation, the cultural silence, and the exhaustion of moving through systems that were not built with her in mind. Her presence offers recognition, encouragement, and the quiet strength of someone who understands.
naræ Steward →
A person or organization who believes culturally grounded support and human dignity matter deeply. They offer what they can: time, skill, resources, advocacy, partnership, or steady presence. Whether visible or behind the scenes, their support helps sustain the work.

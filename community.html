Ways to Be Part

Whether you are seeking care, offering support, or exploring collaboration — there is a place for you in naræ+.

Who belongs here.

naræ+ Fellow
An immigrant or culturally diverse woman navigating breast or gynecologic cancer, often carrying not only a diagnosis, but also the weight of language, migration, family roles, and the experience of feeling unseen. She comes to naræ+ to be received fully, as she is. She is at the heart of why naræ+ exists.
naræ+ Companion
A naræ+ Fellow who, when she feels ready, may choose to walk beside another woman beginning a similar journey. Companion is not a separate role, but something a naræ+ Fellow may grow into in her own time. In walking beside another, her own healing may deepen.
naræ+ Steward
A person or organization who helps sustain naræ+ through service, generosity, partnership, advocacy, or support behind the scenes. Stewards may include other cancer survivors, including those outside the communities naræ+ is specifically centered on, as well as friends, professionals, volunteers, and partners who believe this work matters and want to help it continue with care.

How support can take shape.

Sometimes a woman’s own healing deepens as she walks beside another.

Time & Presence
Showing up, listening, walking beside.
Language & Cultural Access
Translation, interpretation, cultural bridging.
Skills & Expertise
Legal, medical, creative, organizational knowledge.
Practical Help
Transportation, meals, coordination, logistics.
Financial Generosity
Sustaining the work so support can continue.

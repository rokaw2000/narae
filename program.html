The 1000 Crane Project

A living expression of hope and solidarity.

A Living Ecosystem

Belong. Renew. Walk Beside.

Belong
Be received as you are.

Care begins with belonging. Through virtual circles, small gatherings, in-person moments, and community connection, support becomes steadier through respect, cultural understanding, and human presence.

Read more

A woman should not have to divide herself in order to be supported. Language, culture, grief, hope, family responsibility, and uncertainty are not side notes here. They are part of the whole person naræ is here to hold.

Renew
Let something return.

Renewal does not happen all at once, and it does not look the same for every woman. Healing is not only survival. It can also be a return to self, to steadiness, to voice, and to what feels possible again.

Read more

For one woman, renewal may look like rest. For another, laughter, courage, creativity, prayer, or trust in her own body again. naræ does not prescribe what healing must be. It simply creates room for it to unfold with gentleness and grace.

Walk Beside
Give forward when ready.

Walk Beside is one of the core healing modalities of naræ. When a woman is ready, walking beside another can become part of how her own healing deepens.

Read more

This is never an obligation. It begins when care received becomes care offered. A woman who has been held may one day become encouragement, companionship, advocacy, or presence for someone else. That cycle of receiving, renewing, and walking beside is what makes naræ a living ecosystem, not simply a program.

What naræ May Include

What support may look like.

Circles & Gatherings

Small virtual or in-person spaces for honest conversation, reflection, and feeling less alone.

Read more
May include cultural sharing, creativity, companionship, or simply space to be present without pressure to perform.
Community Events

Gentle gatherings for connection, remembrance, encouragement, and cultural expression.

Read more
Shared meals, tea, reflection, beauty, sensory care — forms of collective support and cultural exchange.
Retreat

An intimate retreat experience for rest, reflection, and being cared for without pressure. By invitation or referral only — not a guaranteed program.

Read more
Keeping retreat small is part of the care itself. It protects emotional safety and allows the experience to remain personal, unhurried, and calm.

naræ is not built for volume. Access may be by invitation or referral. That is part of how the spirit, trust, and dignity of this work are held.

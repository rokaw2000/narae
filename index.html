Symbol & Meaning

Why the Crane

A symbol of grace, resilience, and return.

The red-crowned crane represents longevity, dignity, and quiet strength.

Read the meaning

At naræ , the crane holds more than symbolism. It reflects the journey of gathering yourself again, slowly and intentionally.

Each woman folds her own crane, not perfectly, but with care. It becomes a reminder that healing is not only about surviving what happened, but about rising and returning to life with presence.

The crane also speaks to reintegration, not just recovery.

That matters to naræ : not only helping women through treatment, but supporting confidence, reconnection, and fuller participation in life again.

Who We Are

A space for women carrying more than what is visible.

naræ centers emotional presence, cultural understanding, and human connection for women navigating breast and gynecologic cancers.

We are especially attentive to women from immigrant and culturally diverse communities who may not always feel seen in traditional support spaces.

Our approach is intentional, human, and grounded in lived experience. We believe healing can continue through connection, confidence, and community, and for some, that journey eventually includes walking beside someone else.

Walk With Someone Else

Some who come to naræ for support find, over time, a quiet desire to walk beside someone else.

Not because they are asked to.
Not because they have to.
But because they understand.

Giving back becomes part of healing, building confidence, connection, and renewed purpose.

What this can look like
  • Sitting with someone during treatment or appointments
  • Checking in through a simple message or call
  • Going for a short walk or sharing quiet time together
  • Offering language support when needed
  • Being present without needing to fix or advise

What This Work Teaches Us

Not all experiences are captured in formal systems.

Some realities are lived quietly, yet they still shape care, confidence, and the way women return to life.

At naræ , we pay attention to what is often unseen: emotional weight, cultural context, quiet resilience, and how confidence can grow through connection and care.

Explore further

Founder Note

Why I Created naræ

Cancer was never an abstract word to me. I grew up knowing what it meant, watching people I loved face it, and not come back.

I never imagined it would come for me. There was no warning, no reason to expect it. And then it did, not once, but twice.

My very first mammogram was a diagnosis.

Read more

During chemotherapy, someone reached out asking for my help. I almost said no. I was very sick. I was tired. It did not feel like the right time.

But something in me said to try. I am glad I did. It gave me purpose. And somewhere in the giving, I found strength I did not know I still had.

As a Korean immigrant, I also know what it feels like to move through a world that was not built for you. I know the quiet habit of not asking for help, of figuring things out alone, of not wanting to be a burden.

I began to wonder where the women like me were. In waiting rooms, in support groups, I did not see them. Not because they did not exist, but because many never entered those spaces at all.

naræ was born from that question, and from connecting the pieces of everything I had lived. It exists so that women can feel understood, supported, and less alone, and when they are ready, pass that care forward and find healing in the giving.

This is not just about getting through. It is about finding your way back, and moving forward with strength.

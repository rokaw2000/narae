A warm, culturally grounded network of support.

In Korean, naræ means wings. The crane symbolizes resilience, grace, and hope — the strength to keep moving forward.

Our mission

naræ International Foundation uplifts immigrant and culturally diverse communities, especially those navigating breast and gynecologic cancers, through restorative experiences, cultural connection, and meaningful acts of support.

Founder’s vision

naræ+ was inspired by founder Jihwan’s personal journey as an immigrant and a breast cancer survivor. The experience revealed how isolating illness can feel when culture, language, and identity create additional barriers.

“Like wings, support and community can help lift someone through seasons that feel too heavy to carry alone.”
Jihwan Baek, Founder

What we hope to do

Restorative experiences

Small retreats and gatherings that create space for rest, reflection, and encouragement.

Cultural connection

Sharing stories, heritage, and creative expression to help people feel rooted and seen.

Community support

Volunteer-centered support that invites participants to give back, transforming lived experience into encouragement and hope for others.

One crane at a time

We are building naræ+ with intention — one thoughtful step, one crane at a time.

Identity & inspiration

About naræ+

The symbolism behind naræ+ is deeply intentional. The name, the red-crowned crane, and the origami form each carry meaning connected to healing, dignity, resilience, and care.

The meaning of naræ

In Korean, naræ (나래) means wings. The name reflects uplift, movement, and the quiet strength it can take to carry on through difficult seasons.

For naræ+, “wings” also suggest hope, protection, and the possibility of beginning again with dignity.

The red-crowned crane

The red-crowned crane was chosen intentionally. It is not simply a decorative bird or a generic crane symbol. It reflects grace, endurance, resilience, dignity, and quiet beauty.

Its presence in the identity of naræ+ speaks to the strength of continuing forward, even through hardship, and the belief that healing can hold both fragility and courage at once.

The meaning of origami

The origami form reflects patience, care, intention, and the beauty of something thoughtfully shaped by hand. It also speaks to transformation: a simple sheet becoming something meaningful through attention, gentleness, and time.

For naræ+, origami represents the idea that healing and community can also be shaped with care, one deliberate act at a time.

Inspiration behind the foundation

naræ+ was inspired by the founder’s journey as an immigrant and breast cancer survivor. That experience revealed how isolating illness, language, culture, and identity can feel when someone is already carrying so much.

The foundation grows from a desire to create warmer, more human, and more culturally grounded forms of support, especially for immigrant and culturally diverse individuals who may feel unseen within traditional systems of care.

“We believe comfort, dignity, and connection can be healing in their own right. Sometimes even a small space of support can help someone breathe again.”
naræ+ guiding spirit

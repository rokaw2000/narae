Founder’s Message

Born from survival. Built with purpose.

naræ International Foundation was shaped by lived experience, cultural memory, and the conviction that women facing breast cancer and gynecologic cancer deserve to feel understood not only through illness, but through identity, language, and dignity.

Founder’s Message

There are experiences in life that do not simply interrupt your path. They change the way you see the world, the way you move through it, and the way you understand what people carry in silence.

I know this personally. I am a two-time breast cancer survivor.

I have lived through the shock, fear, and uncertainty that cancer brings, and I continue to live through the challenges that survivorship can still hold. Along that journey, I received support that was meaningful and generous, and I remain deeply grateful for it.

But even in those moments of help, I also recognized something that stayed with me. I did not often see many women who looked like me, sounded like me, or carried the same cultural layers that I did.

As a Korean immigrant, I understood that asking for support is not always simple. In my early years in the United States, even small interactions could feel intimidating. I remember times when speaking to a store cashier required courage because I worried about whether my words would come out the right way. That feeling never fully left me. It helped me understand why many women like me may hesitate to seek support, even when they need it deeply.

Sometimes the barrier is not a lack of strength. Sometimes it is the quiet disconnect of not feeling fully understood. Something feels missing. Something feels distant. And when breast cancer or gynecologic cancer enters a woman’s life, those additional layers can make an already difficult journey even heavier.

That is why I created naræ.

naræ International Foundation was created to support immigrant women and culturally diverse women facing breast cancer and gynecologic cancer, recognizing that their journeys may carry additional layers of language, cultural difference, fear, and isolation.

I have also been a lifelong volunteer and, in many ways, born to public service. During chemotherapy, I came to understand something that changed me. Helping others gave me purpose. It made me feel alive at a time when so much felt uncertain. That is when I realized that purpose itself can become part of healing.

Much of cancer support is one-directional. Women receive support, which is deeply important, and often the story ends there. But I believe there is more. I believe that, when a woman feels ready, giving back in whatever way feels meaningful to her can also become a healing modality. It can restore dignity. It can reconnect her to purpose. It can help turn survival into something sustaining.

At naræ+, that full circle matters. Women may receive encouragement, care, and community, and in time, if they choose, they may also give back however they see fit — through culture, language, creativity, wisdom, service, or simple presence. That is one of the core ideas behind this foundation.

naræ International Foundation is a Maryland-based nonprofit. Maryland is my home, and I am proud to begin this journey from the place I call home. At the same time, as the foundation grows, its programs and community support may extend beyond Maryland wherever women may need culturally aware care, encouragement, and connection.

My hope is that naræ will become a place where women who might otherwise feel unseen can feel recognized, supported, and less alone — and where healing can grow into purpose, and purpose can continue outward into community.

Jihwan M. Baek
Founder, naræ International Foundation

“I believe there is healing not only in receiving care, but also in discovering that helping others can help us feel alive again.”

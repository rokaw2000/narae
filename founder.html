From the Founder

Why I created naræ+

naræ International Foundation was born from lived experience, cultural identity, and the belief that no woman should have to navigate illness feeling unseen or alone.

I am a two-time breast cancer survivor. Cancer changed the way I see life, hardship, and the quiet strength people carry every day.

During my own journey, I received meaningful support that helped me keep moving forward. I remain deeply grateful for that care. At the same time, I noticed something that stayed with me: I did not often see many women facing the additional challenges that can come with navigating illness across language, culture, and unfamiliar systems.

As a Korean immigrant, I understand how difficult it can be to ask for help. In my early years in the United States, even simple conversations could feel intimidating. I still remember moments when speaking to a store cashier required courage because I was unsure whether my words would come out the right way.

Experiences like that shaped my understanding of how language barriers, cultural differences, and unfamiliarity can make a hard season even harder. When breast cancer or gynecologic cancer enters a woman’s life, those added layers can deepen fear, isolation, and hesitation.

naræ was created with those realities in mind. It was created to support immigrant women and culturally diverse women from many backgrounds who may be carrying more than illness alone.

I have also been a lifelong volunteer and public servant. During chemotherapy, I realized something that changed my understanding of healing: helping others gave me purpose. It reminded me that life still held meaning beyond illness.

Much of cancer support understandably focuses on receiving help. That care is essential. But I also believe healing can continue in another direction. When women feel ready, giving back in whatever way feels natural to them can also become part of restoration.

At naræ+, women may receive encouragement and support, and in time they may also choose to share culture, language, creativity, wisdom, or simple presence with others. Care does not have to end with one person. It can keep moving forward.

naræ International Foundation is proudly based in Maryland, the place I now call home. As the community grows, I hope its reach can extend beyond Maryland wherever women may benefit from culturally aware encouragement, connection, and support.

My hope is that naræ becomes a place where women who might otherwise feel overlooked can feel understood, supported, and less alone.

Jihwan M. Baek Founder, naræ International Foundation

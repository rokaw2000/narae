Founder’s Message

naræ+ was born from hardship, gratitude, and the belief that giving back can be part of healing.

This foundation is deeply personal to me. It was shaped by survival, cultural identity, public service, and a growing conviction that compassion and purpose can help people rise again.

A personal note

Why I created naræ+

naræ+ was created from the most difficult and transformative chapters of my life.

I am a two-time breast cancer survivor, an immigrant, and a proud public servant. Those parts of my life have shaped how I understand hardship, dignity, resilience, and the deep human need to feel seen and supported.

Living through illness changed me. It made me more aware of how heavy life can become when fear, uncertainty, culture, language, and identity all meet at the same time. It also taught me that healing is not only physical. Sometimes it begins when someone feels understood. Sometimes it begins with kindness. Sometimes it begins when a person rediscovers meaning after pain.

naræ+ grew out of that understanding. I wanted to create something rooted in care, cultural respect, and purpose. Not something rushed or generic, but something thoughtful. Something that could hold compassion and strength at once.

What I believe

Healing is not only about receiving

One of the strongest lessons I have learned is that healing does not always come only from being helped. It can also come from helping others.

Giving back, serving, creating space for others, and offering encouragement can become part of rebuilding a life. Purpose can restore something that hardship tries to take away. It can return a sense of direction, dignity, and connection.

That belief is at the heart of naræ+. I believe support should not stop at comfort alone. It should also make room for belonging, contribution, and the quiet restoration that comes when people are reminded that their lives still carry value and meaning.

Why it matters

Why this foundation feels different to me

naræ+ is not an idea I chose because it sounded beautiful. It came from lived experience, cultural memory, and a long journey of learning how to keep moving forward.

As an immigrant, I know that care can feel different when language, culture, and identity are part of the experience. As a survivor, I know that strength is often quieter than people imagine. As a public servant, I believe deeply in responsibility, service, and showing up for others with integrity.

These parts of my life come together in naræ+. They shape a vision of support that is more human, more culturally grounded, and more aware of the whole person.

The vision

What I hope naræ+ will become

I hope naræ+ becomes a place where people feel dignity instead of invisibility, warmth instead of isolation, and encouragement instead of silence.

I hope it grows into a foundation that offers meaningful connection, thoughtful support, and opportunities for people not only to receive care, but also to reclaim purpose.

I want naræ+ to honor culture, resilience, craftsmanship, and the beauty of rising again after hardship. Like the origami crane in our identity, this foundation is being shaped carefully, fold by fold. Like embroidery, it is being built thread by thread.

It is still growing, but it is growing with intention.

With hope and purpose,
Jihwan Baek
Founder, Executive Director, and Board Chair

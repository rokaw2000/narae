From the Founder

Why I created naræ

A personal message about cancer, healing, cultural identity, and the question that started it all.

Cancer. I grew up knowing what that word meant. I watched people I loved face it, and some of them did not come back.

I never imagined it would come for me. There was no genetic warning. No reason to expect it.

And then it did — not once, but twice. My very first mammogram was a diagnosis.

During chemotherapy, someone reached out asking for my help. I almost said no — I was very sick, I was tired, and it didn't seem like the right time. But something inside told me to try.

I'm glad I did. It gave me a good distraction. It gave me renewed purpose. And somewhere in that giving, I discovered strength I didn't know I still had.

That was the moment I understood that giving — volunteerism — was part of healing for me. Not a side effect of recovery. The thing itself.

That moment stayed with me. It made me start asking a deeper question about healing — and about who is able to access support in the first place.

As a Korean immigrant, I know what it feels like to navigate a world that wasn't built for you. I know the quiet habit of not asking for help — of figuring things out alone, of not wanting to be a burden to anyone. I still remember when speaking to a store cashier required great courage. That feeling stays with you longer than you expect.

And then I wondered — where were the women like me? In the waiting rooms, in the support groups, I didn't see them. Not because they didn't exist — but because many immigrant and culturally diverse women never enter those spaces. The language barriers, the cultural differences, the deeply ingrained habit of staying quiet — it all keeps them away.

naræ was born from that question — and from connecting the dots of everything I had lived. It exists so that those women can feel understood, supported, and less alone. And when they are ready, they too may choose to pass that forward — discovering strength in the act of giving.

That question became naræ.

JB
Jihwan M. Baek Founder, naræ International Foundation

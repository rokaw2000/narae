Founder’s Message

The heart behind naræ+.

naræ International Foundation was shaped by lived experience, cultural memory, and the conviction that women deserve more than to endure hardship alone. They deserve dignity, encouragement, meaningful connection, and a place where care feels human.

Why this vision began

naræ began with the understanding that illness, displacement, emotional isolation, and difficult life seasons can quietly change the way a person moves through the world. For immigrant women and culturally diverse women especially, those experiences can also carry layers of language, identity, cultural difference, and silence that are often unseen.

This foundation was envisioned as a response to that reality: a thoughtful place where women can feel seen not only for what they are enduring, but for who they are. A place shaped by care, culture, and the belief that support can restore dignity and connection.

naræ+ also holds special sensitivity for women navigating breast cancer and gynecologic cancer, while remaining open to the wider emotional, cultural, and community needs that often surround those journeys.

Healing with dignity

The founder’s vision is rooted in the belief that healing is not only clinical. It can also be emotional, cultural, relational, and creative. It can grow through writing, music, language, storytelling, cultural sharing, and the quiet reassurance that someone is not alone.

Community with purpose

naræ is meant to be more than a place of comfort. It is meant to become a living circle of encouragement where connection deepens into purpose, and purpose opens the possibility of giving back.

Giving back as part of healing

A central part of the founder’s vision is that healing does not always end with receiving support. For many women, healing grows stronger when purpose returns. When they feel ready, they may choose to give back by sharing their culture, language, music, writing, story, encouragement, or simple presence with others in the naræ community.

In this way, naræ+ becomes a full circle. Women are not only supported. They may also become part of the support. What was received with care may one day be shared with care.

That spirit of giving back is not an obligation. It is an invitation. It is one of the ideas at the center of naræ: that meaning, service, and connection can help people rise again.

“At naræ+, healing is not only about receiving care. When a woman feels ready, sharing her culture, language, creativity, encouragement, or presence can become part of healing itself.”

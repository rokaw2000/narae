Founder’s Message

naræ' was born from hardship, gratitude, and the belief that giving back can be part of healing.

This foundation is deeply personal to me. It was shaped by survival, cultural identity, public service, and a conviction that compassion and purpose can help people rise again.

A personal note

Why I created naræ'

naræ' grew out of the most difficult and transformative chapters of my life.

I am a two-time breast cancer survivor, an immigrant, and a proud public servant. Those experiences shaped how I understand hardship, dignity, resilience, and the deep human need to feel seen and supported.

Illness changed the way I see healing. I learned that healing is not only physical. Sometimes it begins when someone feels understood. Sometimes it begins with kindness. Sometimes it begins when a person rediscovers meaning after pain.

I wanted to build something thoughtful and humane, rooted in care, cultural respect, and purpose.

What I believe

Healing can include giving back

One of the clearest lessons of my life is that healing does not come only from receiving help. It can also grow through helping others.

Service, encouragement, and creating space for others can become part of rebuilding a life. Purpose can restore direction and connection.

That belief is at the heart of naræ'.

The vision

What I hope this foundation becomes

I hope naræ' becomes a place where people feel seen, supported, and encouraged as they move through hardship and renewal.

I hope it grows into a foundation that offers thoughtful connection, meaningful care, and opportunities for people not only to receive support, but also to reclaim purpose.

Like the origami crane in our identity, this foundation is being shaped carefully, fold by fold. Like embroidery, it is being built thread by thread.

With hope and purpose,
Jihwan Baek
Founder, Executive Director, and Board Chair

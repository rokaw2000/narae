1000 Crane Project

The 1000 Crane Project

The 1000 Crane Project reflects hope, healing, remembrance, and the quiet strength of community. It is a symbolic expression of care, intention, and encouragement.

Why 1000 cranes

The image of a thousand origami cranes carries deep meaning across cultures and has often been associated with hope, healing, peace, endurance, and heartfelt wishes.

Within naræ+, the project reflects the power of small, deliberate acts of care coming together into something larger, more beautiful, and deeply meaningful.

What it represents for naræ+

The 1000 Crane Project reflects the belief that healing can be strengthened through intention, tenderness, memory, and community connection.

Each crane can symbolize encouragement, solidarity, remembrance, prayer, hope, or a simple act of love offered to someone navigating a difficult season.

How the community may participate

As naræ+ grows, the 1000 Crane Project may become a way for members, participants, supporters, and loved ones to contribute symbolic acts of care to the foundation’s mission.

Future participation details may include folding cranes, sharing stories, honoring survivors, remembering loved ones, or creating collaborative installations shaped by community meaning.

1000 Crane Project

The 1000 Crane Project

The 1000 Crane Project reflects hope, remembrance, healing, and the quiet strength of many small acts of care gathered together.

The meaning

Why 1000 cranes

The image of a thousand origami cranes carries deep symbolic meaning and has long been associated with hope, healing, peace, endurance, and heartfelt wishes.

For naræ', it reflects the belief that small, deliberate acts of care can gather into something larger, more beautiful, and deeply meaningful.

What it represents

A project rooted in care and remembrance

The 1000 Crane Project reflects intention, tenderness, memory, and community connection.

Each crane may symbolize encouragement, solidarity, remembrance, prayer, hope, or a simple act of love offered to someone moving through a difficult season.

Future participation

How the community may take part

As naræ' grows, the project may become a way for Naræ Fellows, community members, supporters, and loved ones to contribute symbolic acts of care.

Future participation may include folding cranes, sharing stories, honoring survivors, remembering loved ones, or helping create collaborative installations shaped by community meaning.

“A single crane is a gesture. A thousand cranes become a shared expression of hope, memory, and care.”

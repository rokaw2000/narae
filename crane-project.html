A Ritual of Care, Prayer & Remembrance

The 1000 Crane Project

Each naræ Fellow folds three origami cranes by hand: one for herself, one for another woman, and one for naræ+. Over time, these cranes become a living expression of care, witness, and shared hope.

Each crane is folded by hand and held with intention, becoming part of a growing collective display of memory, care, and belonging.

A living ritual

Three cranes, one act of care

Within the naræ+ retreat experience, each Fellow is invited to fold three origami cranes by hand. It is a quiet act, unhurried and simple, requiring only patience, presence, and intention, the same qualities that often shape healing itself.

Each crane moves in a different direction: inward toward herself, outward toward another woman, and forward toward the community. Together, the three form one complete gesture of care.

Every crane is kept

A record held with care

naræ+ keeps the collective cranes as a visible and tangible record of the women who have moved through this community: their journeys, their care for others, and their presence here.

Over time, the collection becomes part of a shared display. When one thousand cranes have been gathered, they will be honored together in a special ceremony, and then the folding continues.

一

One for herself

A crane folded in acknowledgment of her own journey, her strength, her survival, and the life she continues to shape fold by fold.

二

One for another

A crane folded in the spirit of another woman, someone she knows, someone she hopes for, or someone she has never met but still carries in her heart.

三

One for naræ+

A crane folded for the foundation and the community it holds, a small offering that becomes part of something larger, shared, and lasting.

Why one thousand

1,000 is not a finish line. It is a prayer.

Folding one thousand cranes has long been associated with prayer, hope, remembrance, and devotion. At naræ+, one thousand cranes represent a full and collective act of witness, honoring the women who folded them, the women they thought of, and the mission they joined.

Reaching one thousand is not the end of the project. It is a moment of reflection, ceremony, and continuation.

The ceremony

A gathering of reflection and remembrance

When the collection reaches one thousand cranes, naræ+ will hold a special ceremony to honor the display and the many quiet acts of care it represents.

It may take shape as an annual gathering, a dedicated installation, or another thoughtfully held moment of reflection. The ceremony is meant to make visible what has been carried gently over time.

How it reflects naræ+

Built gradually, with care

The 1000 Crane Project reflects the way naræ+ itself is being built. Meaningful support is rarely created all at once. It grows through repeated acts of intention, presence, trust, and human connection.

A crane is made fold by fold. So is a life rebuilt. So is this foundation.

Who shapes this project

Held within the retreat community

This project grows through the hands and stories of naræ Fellows and those directly connected to the retreat experience. That helps preserve the intimacy, meaning, and integrity of the project as it slowly unfolds.

It is not simply a display. It is a communal archive of care.

"Some acts are small enough to hold in the hand, yet large enough to carry memory, encouragement, and hope forward."
naræ+ guiding spirit

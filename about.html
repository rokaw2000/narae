About naræ+

The name naræ and its symbols reflect the foundation’s belief in dignity, resilience, cultural care, and the possibility of rising again.

The word naræ is inspired by the Korean word for wing. A wing suggests movement, lift, and the possibility of rising again after hardship. For many women facing breast cancer or gynecologic cancer, the journey can feel heavy and isolating. naræ represents the hope that encouragement, understanding, and community can help women find strength again.

The symbol of the red-crowned crane carries deep cultural meaning across East Asia. It represents longevity, resilience, grace, and enduring hope. In the identity of naræ, the crane reflects dignity carried through difficult seasons of life.

The plus sign in naræ+ symbolizes something added — support, compassion, connection, and possibility. The red tones represent courage, survival, and the lived realities of cancer. The accent color introduces renewal and forward movement, expressing the belief that healing can continue beyond illness.

Together, these elements express the spirit of naræ: care that honors culture, recognizes resilience, and creates space for women to find encouragement, community, and purpose.

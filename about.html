Identity & Meaning

About naræ+

The meaning behind naræ+ is personal, cultural, and intentional. The name, the red-crowned crane, the origami form, the embroidered character of the mark, and the raised plus symbol all reflect resilience, dignity, care, craftsmanship, and the determination to rise again.

The name

The meaning of naræ

In Korean, naræ (나래) means wings.

Wings suggest lift, shelter, movement, and the quiet strength it takes to keep going through difficult seasons.

That meaning is central to this foundation. It reflects resilience, care, and the belief that people can still move forward after hardship.

The heart of the identity

A symbol shaped with intention

naræ+ is not a decorative name or mark. Every part of the identity was chosen with care.

Together, the crane, the folded form, the embroidered texture, and the raised plus symbol express dignity, prayer, craftsmanship, and the quiet courage of rising again.

The identity is meant to feel gentle yet strong, refined yet human.

The red-crowned crane

The red-crowned crane is one of the most revered birds in East Asian culture. Across Korea, Japan, and China, it has long been associated with longevity, fidelity, grace, integrity, and noble endurance.

It is also a vulnerable yet powerful bird. Tall, watchful, and elegant, it carries strength without harshness.

The crane reflects the kind of strength this foundation honors: not loud, but steady.

The meaning of origami

Across many Asian cultures, origami cranes are folded as gestures of hope, prayer, blessing, and heartfelt wishing.

An origami crane is never only decorative. It carries intention. Someone took time to shape it by hand.

The bird in the naræ+ identity appears in folded form because it reflects transformation, shaped carefully fold by fold.

The embroidered mark

The naræ+ mark is presented in an embroidered style as a reflection of craftsmanship.

Embroidery is built patiently, thread by thread, with discipline, intention, and care. In the same way, this foundation is being built thoughtfully rather than hurriedly.

The mark carries the spirit of something made by hand, not mass-produced.

The symbol

The meaning of the raised plus

The plus in naræ+ represents more compassion, more connection, more dignity, and more room for people to feel seen and supported.

Its four lines carry additional meaning. Three pink lines honor those affected by breast cancer. The teal line honors those facing gynecologic cancers.

Three pink lines represent breast cancer.
One teal line represents gynecologic cancers.
What it stands for

A foundation shaped by care and meaning

naræ+ brings together cultural memory, lived experience, and purposeful service.

It seeks to honor the whole person: their story, identity, hardship, resilience, and capacity to give something back to the world.

At the heart of the foundation is a simple belief: giving back can be part of healing.

“We believe comfort, dignity, and connection can be healing in their own right. Sometimes even a small space of support can help someone breathe again.”
naræ+ guiding spirit

Identity & Meaning

About Naræ+

The meaning behind naræ+ is cultural, personal, and intentional. The name, the red-crowned crane, the origami form, the embroidered character of the mark, and the plus symbol all reflect resilience, dignity, prayer, craftsmanship, and the determination to rise again.

The name

The meaning of naræ

In Korean, naræ (나래) means wings.

Wings suggest lift, protection, movement, and the quiet strength it takes to continue through difficult seasons. They hold the image of something once weighed down gathering itself and rising again.

For this foundation, that meaning is deeply personal. naræ+ was shaped by the lived experience of a two-time breast cancer survivor, an immigrant, and a public servant who believes that giving back can be part of healing.

Why it exists

Where the foundation comes from

naræ+ grew from the desire to create support that feels more human, more culturally grounded, and more aware of the whole person.

It was shaped by the understanding that illness can become even heavier when layered with language, culture, identity, and the feeling of not being fully seen.

At its heart is a simple conviction: healing is not only about surviving. It can also be strengthened by purpose, connection, and service.

The red-crowned crane

The red-crowned crane is one of the most respected birds in East Asian culture. Across Korea, Japan, and China, it has long been associated with longevity, fidelity, integrity, grace, and noble endurance.

It is also a vulnerable yet powerful bird. Tall, watchful, and elegant, it carries strength without aggression. Its presence reflects resilience, steadiness, awareness, and dignity under pressure.

That alignment matters to naræ+. The crane represents the kind of strength this foundation honors: not loud, but unwavering.

The meaning of origami

For many people across Asia, origami cranes are folded as gestures of hope, prayer, blessing, and heartfelt wishing.

Growing up, receiving an origami crane was not simply decorative. It carried care. It suggested that someone had shaped a small act of intention for you.

The bird in the naræ+ identity appears in origami form because it reflects transformation: something shaped fold by fold until it becomes capable of symbolizing flight again.

The embroidered mark

The naræ+ mark is presented in an embroidered style as a reflection of craftsmanship.

Embroidery is created patiently, thread by thread, with discipline, intention, and care. In the same way, this foundation is being built thoughtfully rather than hurriedly.

The logo carries the spirit of something crafted by hand, just as the work of building meaningful support must also be shaped with care.

The symbol

The meaning of the plus

The plus in naræ+ represents more: more compassion, more connection, more dignity, and more room for people to feel seen and supported.

It also reflects what can come after hardship. Not only survival, but renewed purpose. Not only receiving care, but extending it outward through service, generosity, and community.

The four lines of the mark carry additional meaning. Three pink lines honor those affected by breast cancer. The teal line honors those facing gynecologic cancers.

Three pink lines represent breast cancer.
One teal line represents gynecologic cancers.
What makes it different

Why naræ+ is distinct

naræ+ is shaped by lived experience, cultural heritage, and a belief in purposeful service.

It does not separate support from identity, dignity, or meaning. It seeks to honor the whole person: their story, culture, hardship, resilience, and capacity to give something back to the world.

At naræ+, giving back is not separate from healing. It is part of it.

“We believe comfort, dignity, and connection can be healing in their own right. Sometimes even a small space of support can help someone breathe again.”
naræ+ guiding spirit

Identity & Meaning

About naræ

The meaning behind naræ is personal, cultural, and intentional. The name, the crane, the origami form, the embroidery texture, and the raised plus mark all reflect resilience, craftsmanship, remembrance, and the belief that we can reshape our lives with care.

The name

The meaning of naræ

In Korean, naræ (나래) means wings.

Wings suggest lift, protection, movement, and the quiet strength it takes to keep going through difficult seasons.

For this foundation, the word holds the image of someone once weighed down — gathering themselves, and rising again.

The heart of the identity

A symbol shaped with intention

naræ is not decorative branding. It is a symbolic identity built around survival, care, memory, and renewal.

The crane, the folded form, the embroidery texture, and the raised plus mark were chosen to express the idea that healing and rebuilding are shaped deliberately — not instantly.

The identity is meant to feel gentle, refined, resilient, and human.

The red-crowned crane

One of the most respected birds in East Asian culture — associated with longevity, grace, integrity, and noble endurance.

Tall, watchful, elegant. It carries strength without aggression. That is the kind of strength naræ honors.

The meaning of origami

Origami cranes are folded across Asian cultures as gestures of hope, prayer, and heartfelt wishing.

A crane is made fold by fold — with patience and intention. That mirrors how people rebuild after hardship.

The embroidery texture

The naræ mark is presented in an embroidered style — shaped thread by thread, by hand, not rushed.

That texture symbolizes how we recreate our lives — carefully, with effort, beauty, and resilience.

The symbol

The meaning of the raised plus

The raised plus in naræ represents more: more compassion, more dignity, more connection, and more room for people to feel seen and supported.

Its four lines carry specific meaning. Three pink lines represent breast cancer. One teal line represents gynecologic cancer.

The mark is small, but not minor. It holds memory, survival, and the belief that even after hardship, life can still expand.

What it stands for

A foundation shaped by care and rebuilding

naræ brings together cultural memory, lived experience, and purposeful service.

It honors the whole person: story, identity, hardship, resilience, and the capacity to begin again.

At its heart is a quiet but firm belief: we are shaped by what we survive — but we also participate in what we become.

"Some things are not rebuilt all at once. They are shaped fold by fold, thread by thread, until a new life begins to take form."
naræ guiding spirit
